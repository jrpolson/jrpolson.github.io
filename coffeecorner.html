Because I loved Indie Coffee so much, I started to work there as a barista, and got an espresso machine to start making my coffees at home. Below I will share my favorite hot and iced latte recipes with instructions:

Salted Vanilla Honey Cinnamon Hot Latte

Ingredients
- Sea salt
- 1 pump vanilla syrup or vanilla bean paste
- 1/2–1 teaspoon honey
- Dash of cinnamon
- 18 g freshly ground espresso beans
- Milk of your choice (I use 2%).
Process
- Brew your fresh espresso over sea salt, vanilla, honey, and cinnamon. It should take 18–24 seconds to brew if correct. Mix to combine.
- To steam your milk correctly, pour just enough until it’s right under the spout. Then, aerate (put the tip of steamer right on surface of the milk) for about 4 seconds if you have a powerful steamer, or 8–10 seconds if not. Then lower the steamer so the tip is fully submerged until your cup is too hot to touch. Milk should be the texture of wet paint!
- Lastly, pour the milk over your espresso in a circular motion from about 5 cm above the cup and with the cup tilted. Then lower the spout almost to the surface and level out your cup to attempt some latte art (it takes practice)!

Iced Maple Butter Latte with Pumpkin Cold Foam

Ingredients
- Sea salt
- 1 tsp maple butter (Trader Joe’s)
- 18 g freshly ground espresso beans
- Milk of your choice (I use 2% or oat)
- Trader Joe’s pumpkin butter
- Pumpkin spice
- Heavy cream
- Chobani sweet cream creamer
- Ice
Process
- Put your maple butter and pinch of sea salt in the bottom of your glass.
- Brew your double shot of espresso over that (or use 2 tsp instant espresso and 1/3 cup hot water).
- Use a frother to blend well.
- Add your milk until the color looks right, then add your ice.
Cold Foam
- Add about a tsp of pumpkin butter to a glass.
- Then add about a tablespoon of heavy cream and a tablespoon of sweet cream creamer.
- Add pumpkin spice.
- Froth until thick but pourable, about 2 minutes.
- Pour over coffee!

Finally... Some Barista Tips!

Milk Matters

The type of milk you choose changes the flavor and texture of your coffee. Whole milk gives creaminess, oat milk is naturally sweet, and 2% is a balanced choice. Learn to steam and froth properly for that café-quality latte foam!

Grind & Brew

Your coffee grind affects taste. Espresso needs a fine grind, drip coffee medium, and cold brew coarse. Adjusting grind size and brew time can help you avoid bitterness or weak flavor.

Temperature Tricks

Hot coffee should be brewed between 195–205°F for the best flavor. Cold brew doesn’t use heat, but longer steeping (12–24 hours) extracts smooth sweetness without bitterness.

Coffee Storage

Keep your beans in an airtight container away from sunlight and moisture. Fresh beans = fresh coffee!
