Meal Prepping + Shopping Guide

Let's Talk Meal Prep!

As a busy college student, I HIGHLY recommend meal prepping — especially if it’s a hectic week. Ironically, I don’t meal prep a ton myself (mostly because I love to cook and make different things each day), but most people don’t have time for that! Still, here’s how I’d suggest going about it if you want to try.

Meal Prep Strategies

  • Start with a plan: Pick 2–3 recipes you can rotate throughout the week. Choose ones with shared ingredients to save money and reduce waste.
  • Cook in batches: Roast a big tray of veggies or bake proteins (like chicken or salmon) at once so you can mix and match meals easily.
  • Use versatile bases: Keep cooked rice, quinoa, or greens ready in containers. You can build bowls, wraps, or salads quickly.
  • Invest in good containers: Separate your meals into portions so you can grab and go without extra prep.
  • Store smart: Keep half your meals in the fridge and freeze the rest to stay fresh all week.
  • Don’t skip sauces: Pesto, tahini, or homemade crema can make a huge difference in keeping meals exciting.
